Out-of-service criteria are the rules an inspector uses to decide whether a truck is too dangerous to keep driving. If a violation meets the criteria, the truck or the driver is placed out of service until the problem is corrected. Knowing them helps you avoid the ones that park a truck.

## What out of service means

It is not just a ticket. An out-of-service order means the truck stops until the problem is fixed. That is downtime stacked on top of the violation, and it usually lands at the worst possible time.

## The common ones

A large share of out-of-service orders come from a few places: brakes that are out of adjustment past the limit or too many defective brakes on the vehicle, flat or cord-showing [tires](/services/tires), broken steering or suspension parts, and certain lighting failures. Brakes alone drive a big piece of the total, which is why we look hard at the [brake system](/services/brake-repair) on every inspection.

## How to stay out of it

Most out-of-service violations come from wear that a regular inspection would have caught. Keep the truck on a [maintenance schedule](/services/preventive-maintenance) and fix the small things before they cross the line.

When we run your [DOT inspection](/dot-inspection) and find something close to the limit, we can repair it in the same stop, so it never becomes an out-of-service order on the road.
