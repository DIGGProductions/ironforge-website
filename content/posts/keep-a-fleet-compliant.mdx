How do you keep a fleet compliant without it eating your week? The trucks all have their own due dates and their own wear, and the paperwork piles up. Here is the approach that works.

## Track every due date

Inspection due dates are per vehicle, so a fleet has them spread across the year. Put them in one place and watch them, or have a shop track them for you. We do this for [fleets](/fleet) so nothing lapses.

## Maintain on a schedule

Most violations are wear. A [preventive maintenance](/services/preventive-maintenance) schedule catches that wear before it becomes an out-of-service item, which keeps trucks on the road and off the report.

## Use one accountable shop

When inspection and repair happen in the same place, nothing falls between two vendors. One shop knows the fleet, keeps the records, and stands behind the work.

## Keep records you can use

A record of what was done and what is due makes the next audit easier and the next budget more predictable.
